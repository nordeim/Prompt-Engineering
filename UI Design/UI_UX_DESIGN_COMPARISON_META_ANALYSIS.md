## 4.3 Universal Truths (Validated Across All 5 Reports)

These four principles apply to ALL projects, regardless of strategic position.

### Truth 1: Intentionality is the Only Differentiator
> iTrust is not "boring." It is intentionally restrained. Kimi is not "flashy." It is intentionally bold. The worst possible state is an unintentional design—a site that is neither clearly trustworthy nor clearly innovative, a victim of default choices and template thinking.

**Application:** Document the "why" for every major design decision. If you cannot articulate it, the decision is not yet intentional.

### Truth 2: Hierarchy is a Sacred Duty
> Both sites, despite their visual differences, have impeccable hierarchy. In iTrust, it's achieved through predictable scale and spacing. In Kimi, it's achieved through dramatic contrast and isolation. But in both, the user never wonders what to look at next.

**Application:** Test your designs by squinting at them. If hierarchy collapses, redesign.

### Truth 3: Whitespace is a Voice, Not an Absence
> In iTrust, whitespace speaks of organization and calm. In Kimi, it speaks of premium isolation and drama. Never treat whitespace as "empty space to fill." Treat it as a deliberate structural material that communicates as powerfully as any typography or image.

**Application:** Before adding any element, ask: "Could whitespace achieve this goal instead?"

### Truth 4: Accessibility is a Mark of Mastery
> Both sites, in their own ways, demonstrate that great designers engineer for inclusion. iTrust does it through inherent simplicity. Kimi must do it through rigorous engineering. But neither uses complexity as an excuse for exclusion.

**Application:** Accessibility is not a constraint—it is a design material. Engineer for it from the start.
