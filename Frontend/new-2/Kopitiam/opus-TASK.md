Design Concept: "Sunshine & Steam"
I'm envisioning a 1970s-inspired Singaporean kopitiam that feels like stepping into your grandmother's kitchen on a sunny morning. The aesthetic should balance warmth with nostalgia, cheerfulness with authenticity—avoiding anything that feels heavy, dated, garish, or overly stylized.

Key Design Decisions:

Typography: Display fonts should lean retro-serif with personality (like Fraunces or Playfair Display), while body text remains clean and warm for readability.

Color Scheme - "Morning Light" Palette: Warm cream backgrounds (#FFF8F0), coral sunrise as primary (#FF7F5C), golden honey as secondary (#E5A040), mint fresh as tertiary (#7FB5A2), coffee brown for neutrals (#5D4037), and pure white for contrast.

1970s Visual Elements: Sunburst and starburst decorative motifs, rounded corners on interactive elements, retro badge and stamp designs to reinforce the vintage kopitiam atmosphere.

Geometric patterns like circles and arches, decorative borders, and vintage-style illustrations complete the aesthetic. The layout should feel asymmetric with overlapping elements, circular motifs, and arch shapes that feel distinctly 70s. Buttons need to be chunky and tactile.

Interaction Design: Hover effects and animations should feel playful with personality—think organic coffee steam effects that enhance rather than distract from the experience.
The nostalgia-driven aesthetic needs to feel cozy without overwhelming users. The design guides them through emotional connection at the hero section, into desire through the menu, building trust via heritage storytelling, and finally prompting action through ordering or visiting.

I'll use SVG patterns instead of images for performance, maintain WCAG AAA accessibility standards, and build with a mobile-first responsive approach using component-based structure. The arch motif becomes the unifying visual element throughout, paired with a morning-to-noon color gradient that creates narrative. Interactive details like steaming coffee cups and floating beans, combined with serif typography that has quirky character, make this design genuinely memorable.

The implementation unfolds across four phases: first establishing semantic HTML5 with CSS Grid/Flexbox for responsive layouts, then layering in retro typography, decorative patterns, and sunburst elements. Next comes the interactive layer—hover states, scroll animations, and coffee steam effects. Finally, I'll ensure accessibility compliance, optimize performance, and test across browsers.
